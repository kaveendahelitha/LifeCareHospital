Cancer

Cancer signs and symptoms can be very similar to those of other childhood illnesses.
It is important to go to your doctor if you're worried. Having cancer brings many changes to a child’s life.
You can help your child by keeping her life as normal as possible. Pediatric cancer is rare, with 1 in every 600 children getting cancer before the age of 15

Pneumonia

Pneumonia is the leading cause of death among children under the age of 5,
with about 800,000 children dying each year.
In many parts of the world, a child dies of pneumonia every 39 seconds.

Ear pain

Ear pain is more common in children and can have many causes,
including ear infections (otitis media), swimming ear (skin infection in the ear canal),
colds or sinus infections, and toothache from the jaw to the ear.
If you have a high fever, both ears are involved, or your child has other symptoms,
your pediatrician may decide that antibiotics are the best treatment.

Cough
